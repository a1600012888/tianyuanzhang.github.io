Localize the strike of the ball while imaging the bottom surface of the table

In this experiment our camera measures the ping-pong table vibrations from below. The measured vibration is used to recover the strike location of the ball mid-paly. A side RGB camera is used to super-impose the recovered impact locations on the table surface. The red circle in the videos below shows the recovered location.



Localize the walking people through transient floor vibrations

When walking, the foot creates floor vibrations that originate at the step- ping location. Therefore, our system can localize the step locations by observing the floor vibrations. We put our cameras on the stairs, looking the vibration of the floor. The gray screen is placed to block the camera's field of view to the walking people.


When using two set of cameras imaging the transient floor vibrations at two set points, we can achieve better localization accuracy. The basic idea is similar to increasing the baseline distance in stereos.
